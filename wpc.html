01 Context

A Top Business School.
A Scattered Community.
A Language Gap.

W. P. Carey is one of the largest and most research-intensive business schools in the United States — ranked top 25 nationally, with faculty publishing in Financial Times–ranked journals on AI, supply chain, accounting, and operations research. Its alumni in China span industries from finance to tech to entrepreneurship.

But on WeChat — the only platform that actually reaches this audience — the school's presence was transactional. Rankings announcements. Event notices. Sporadic posts with no connective tissue, no editorial voice, no reason to keep reading.

The WeChat ecosystem itself was also shifting: follower growth across official accounts was slowing, subscription lists were collapsed by default, and algorithm-driven discovery was replacing direct subscriptions. Competing business schools — London Business School, Oxford Saïd — were already moving to Video Accounts; HKU had 1,475 RedNote followers. WPC had neither.

"The question wasn't what to publish. It was what kind of publication to become — and what kind of community to build."

03 Architecture

Five Pillars.
One Coherent Voice.

I structured the content operation around five distinct but interlocking pillars — each serving a different reader need, together forming a platform identity that the audience recognizes and trusts.

I

Research Translation

凯瑞快讯 · Research-to-Narrative

Faculty publish world-class research on AI in supply chain, behavioral economics, real estate technology. Almost none of it reaches the people who could use it. I built 凯瑞快讯 — a recurring newsletter format that took one faculty insight per issue and made it accessible, relevant, and worth sharing. Each issue: one professor, one finding, one real-world implication.

Why it works: positions the school as a source of useful thinking, not just credentials — and gives faculty a reason to engage.

II

AI & Business Intelligence

AI与商业洞察 · Jacob Said Series

As AI reshaped every conversation about business, I made it a consistent editorial thread — not trend-chasing, but structured thinking grounded in faculty expertise. Will ChatGPT replace accountants? How should managers lead in an AI era? What does "human-centered" management actually look like? These pieces generated the most substantive reader comments — alumni writing full paragraphs in response, not just reactions.

Why it works: the school has genuine expertise here. Making it visible and specific creates differentiation that rankings can't provide.

III

Alumni Stories & Spotlights

Sun Devil 变身系列 · 261 avg views

The data was unambiguous: alumni content delivered 261 average views per article — highest of any content type, on just 12% of output. I developed recurring story formats: Sun Devil 变身 (individual transformation narratives from '15 DBA graduates to current undergrads), Alumni Hall of Fame profiles, and deeper career essays. Each story was reported and written with editorial care — not PR polish, not bullet-point profiles.

Why it works: prospective students want to see futures, not statistics. Alumni want to see themselves. Same content, two audiences.

IV

Rankings & Institutional Voice

排名叙事 · Context Over Announcement

Rankings matter in China — but only if they mean something. When WPC broke into the national top 25 undergraduate, or when faculty appeared in the Financial Times, I didn't just announce it. I contextualized: what does this ranking actually measure? What does it reveal about how the school teaches? What does a WPC degree mean in concrete career terms? I turned institutional wins into editorial arguments for why this place is different.

Why it works: authority without context is noise. The WPC rankings post hit 578 views in FY25 — top article of the year.

V

Career & Professional Development

职业发展 · Practical Thinking Series

Survey data confirmed what content performance suggested: career development content is the #3 most-wanted content type (40/66 respondents). I curated and wrote this content as a strategic advisor would — stakeholder mapping for new graduates, what the jump from campus to corporation actually feels like, how to navigate identity in a post-pandemic job market. Not a career center pamphlet. A thinking partner.

Why it works: being useful to someone's actual life — not just their alumni identity — builds the kind of trust that makes community possible.

The 31.3% open rate — against an industry benchmark of <5% — is not a formatting trick. 07 Thinking

What This Work
Is Actually About

Business schools need translators, not just marketers.

The research happening inside WPC — on AI decision-making, supply chain fragility, behavioral economics, real estate technology — is genuinely important. The problem is the gap between who produces it and who could use it. I'm interested in closing that gap: not by dumbing things down, but by finding the human story inside the technical argument. 凯瑞快讯 is one answer to that problem. There are others.

Community is infrastructure, not an outcome.

Most organizations treat community as something that happens after you build an audience. The data says otherwise: alumni content drives 2× the engagement of institutional content, on 12% of the output. 73% of surveyed alumni are willing to help promote content or share their stories. The audience isn't passive. The question is whether the platform gives them something worth showing up for — and something worth passing on.

China's professional audience wants substance, not softness.

There's a tendency to make content for Chinese professional audiences either purely practical (career tips, test prep) or purely celebratory (rankings wins, alumni success). The comments on WPC's AI and management pieces suggest something else: this audience wants to think alongside the institution — about where business is going, about what leadership looks like in an AI era, about what a business education is actually for. I tried to give them that.

Platform identity is a compounding asset.

By the time you've built a recognizable editorial voice — consistent formats, consistent perspective, consistent quality — you've created something that can't be quickly copied. 凯瑞快讯 isn't just a newsletter. It's a standing promise: every issue, one piece of research made worth reading. That promise is the asset. And it's what makes the next platform expansion — Video Account, RedNote — possible without starting from zero.
